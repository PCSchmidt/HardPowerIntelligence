## The three candidate surfaces

Each desk page keeps the same cited brief underneath; the archetype is the **hero mechanic** that
leads *above* the read — the BLUF-and-visualization layer that's missing today. (Derived from the
five persona surface-bundles in [PERSONAS.md](PERSONAS.md), collapsed to three mockable mechanics.)

| Fig. | Archetype | Hero mechanic | Serves | Build risk | The bet |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **A** | Convergence Map | Interactive entity/theme graph; node → Entity360 + cited appearances | P1 hedge-fund, P3 corp-dev | **High** | "Explore" beats "read the list" — *if* the graph stays legible at real density. Highest wow, highest risk. |
| **B** | Capital & Catalyst Dashboard | Tracked-capital total + magnitude bars + catalyst calendar + source strip | P2 infra-PE, P4 policy | **Medium** | Mostly composition of data we already compute; D137/D138 just made the capital tile honest enough to headline. Best effort-to-payoff. |
| **C** | Narrative Convergence Homepage | Editorial "state of the desk" BLUF + a few glance tiles, minimal graph | P5 generalist | **Low** | If people want to be *told* not to explore, this wins and is nearly free. B answers: is "told" enough to pay for? |

**Reading the spread:** the axis is **explore (A) → track (B) → be told (C)**. A is the differentiated
"wow" but the riskiest/most expensive; C is the safe, cheap extension of today's reader; B is the
middle option most *unlocked by recent work* (the tracked-vs-projected honesty fix, D138).

## How it feeds the Phase-C decision

In each B2/B3 conversation, note which plate the person physically reacts to, score their resemblance
to the personas (PERSONAS.md worksheet), tally the surface votes, and the winner — ties broken toward
lower build risk (C < B < A) — is the Phase-C **C1** build target. Triangulate the vote tally against
raw enthusiasm; a strong reaction to one mock outweighs a marginal vote lead.
